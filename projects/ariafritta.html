Following a research , we discovered that people usally think about designers as publicists or illustrators, but actually designer have both the skills and the attitude to be leaders and mediators, not just tools for someone else.
"Aria Fritta Pro" is a fictional product that my group and I made for the final step of the Laboratorio di Sintesi at Politecnico di Milano. We designed a digital strategy with the goal of disclosing the role of the designer in our society. The campaign uses an ironical tone of voice to represent the "skills" of the designer as a spray that can fix problems. The message is that design is not istantanious nor futile and can be particularly useful in contexts of social innovation.

In Italy there are thousands of small local museums that are often badly managed or unable to establish a relationship with the citizenship. We think that this is the perfect context where a designer can operate because it can be a hotbed for innovative ideas and also can generate a new cultural and social value and give it back the community.
The website explain the whole case study which the campaign is based on.
Our targets were designers and those in charge to manage the museums, so mainly councilors and city officials, that usually don't know what exactly a designer can do. The campaign started with 3 main touchpoint that brought users on the Aria Fritta website.
